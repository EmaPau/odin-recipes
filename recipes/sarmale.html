Sarmale Recipe

Description

These traditional sarmals are not missing from Romanians' Holiday tables. Sarmals are made for Christmas, New Year, the celebration of different saints and even Easter (in some areas). The most beloved Romanian sarmăluțe are the ones with minced pork (or pork-beef mix), wrapped in thin sheets of sauerkraut.

Ingredients

  • 500 g minced pork (fattier)
  • 1 medium onion chopped
  • 50 g of rice
  • 1 tablespoon of oil
  • salt pepper
  • dried thyme, a little paprika
  • 2 medium sauerkraut

Steps

  1. Step One

    2. I chose 2 pickled cabbage from the barrel. It must have thin sheets that roll easily. I tasted them and they were ok. Not too sour and not too salty.

  2. Step Two

    3. I prepared my minced pork, smoked meat (kaiser) and raw pork brisket.

  3. Step Three

    4. This is the raw pork brisket I was talking about. I cut it first into slices and then into smaller pieces. So did the smoked kaiser next to him.

  4. Step four

    5. I finely chopped an onion and boiled it in a saucepan with a spoonful of oil and a pinch of salt. I didn't let it brown, just become glassy. I also put the raw (dry) rice on top of it and cooked it for these 2 minutes on low heat, stirring constantly so that it does not stick. I turned off the heat and pulled the kettle aside to cool.

  5. Step Five

    6. I placed the minced meat in a deep bowl and tossed the (chilled) onion rice over it. I seasoned well with salt, freshly ground black pepper and dried thyme. I also put 2 good teaspoons of sweet paprika which gives flavor and color

  6. Step Six

    7. I kneaded everything well with my hands to make sure I had a homogeneous composition. Some now also add water or tomato juice, but I don't do that. Under no circumstances do NOT add egg because we are not making meatballs or sausages that need to be "tied". The filling does not run out of the wires!

    I told you above that cabbage leaves are cut with a knife by removing the thickened portions. I put a spoonful of filling on each piece of cabbage leaf and rolled the wire. Then, with my finger, I pushed the ends of it inwards. And that's it! My sarmals were about 7-8 cm long and about 3.5 cm in diameter..

  7. Step Seven

    8. I prepared my thick tomato juice, chopped cabbage and meats (raw pork belly and smoked meat).

  8. Step Eight

    9. I cooked part of these traditional sarmals in a pot on the stove and the rest in the oven, in a ceramic form with a lid. I show you the last ones. On the bottom of the mold I put a chopped onion (raw, unseasoned), 2 tablespoons of oil, 2 sprigs of thyme and a layer of chopped sauerkraut (the spines from the leaves). This was followed by a layer of smoked and raw pork brisket and a layer of sarmale. Among them I filled the spaces with meat and kaiser. I continued like this with the sarmales until 3 cm below the lip of the ceramic pot.

  9. Step Nine

    10. On top I finished with a layer of chopped cabbage and 2 sprigs of thyme. Whoever wants can also put a bay leaf. I poured thick tomato juice and cabbage pulp diluted with a little water (juice from sauerkraut). The sausages must be covered with liquid.

  10. Step Ten

    11. I put the lid on the dish and put it in the oven at 180 C for 30 minutes and then at 150 C for the next 2.5 hours. In total I cooked these traditional sarmals for 3 hours with the lid on. I topped up their sauce with a cup of warm water every hour (as needed). At the end, there must be sauce up to the middle of the bowl.
